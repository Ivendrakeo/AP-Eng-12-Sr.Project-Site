About ARPA

The internet has come a long way from where it started back in 1967 when the project was unofficially started. Like all modern communication technologies, the internet originated from a government program. Its funding derived from a $19,000 grant to the Advanced Research Project Agency [ARPA] and the project was originally intended to provide universities and colleges easy access to shared research and information. The organization quickly realized that the easiest way to accomplish their goal was by using computers as a medium to share information and therefore started the internet program.

Their Part in Development

The ARPA after receiving their grant proceeded to develop the early infrastructure for the internet. They started by controlling access points and had a monopoly on the network. As the project progressed, ARPA developed protocols to keep their technology standardized. In 1981 access to the ARPANET was expanded when the company received a huge grant to continue development. In 1982, the Internet protocol (TCP/IP) was introduced and became the standard networking protocol on the internet. In the early 1980s ARPA developed the first prototypes, which created connections from research and education organizations. ARPANET was disbanded 10 years later after the rapid growth of their technology and completion of their goal.
