Browsers Routers Networks Network Addresses

IP Address

Every device that connects to the internet directly is given a unique number to differentiate it from everything else. This number is like an address and is referred to as an IP number. IP is only used for devices that interact directly with internet traffic like routers. Devices on a router's network act like people who live at an address, they each get their own unique sub-numbers to identify them at the address. IP addresses are probably the most piece of information when routing data through the internet because it ensures all the packets are sent to the same local destination.

Subnet Mask

Subnet Mask takes the IP address and subdivides it into network and host names. Device IP addresses have trailing bytes or extra zeros at the end reserved just for this reason. The Subnet Mask will tell the device what ports are reserved for outgoing and which are reserved for incoming traffic. This allows routers to multitask and differentiate what can go out, and what can come in.

MAC Address

MAC addresses work in conjunction with IP addresses in order to get packets of information to their desired location. Whereas IP addresses sends packets over the internet to another address, MAC makes sure it is routed on a local level correctly. This usually takes place over a physical harlined cord or via Wi-Fi connection from a router to a device and is used as a last step verification before the information is allowed into the device.
