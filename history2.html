Overview ARPA Internet Today

Today

Since the mid-1990s, the Internet has had a revolutionary impact on modern life. The rise of instant communication by electronic mail, instant messaging, voice over Internet, telephone calls, interactive video calls, and the World Wide Web with its forums, blogs, social networking, and shopping sites are just a few of the features offered by modern internet. The research and education, which was the original intention of the technology, continues to develop. Speeds and ease of access continue to shape the modern goals. Today, the Internet continues to grow, with ever expanding amounts of online information, commerce, entertainment, and the more recent social networking.

Market Growth

Originally when the internet was commercialized, the majority of internet traffic was regional because it was reserved for the privileged and technologically inclined. In modern day, the internet is available on a variety of devices and platforms. Staying connected is very important for a lot of people and having the internet available on all sorts of devices has helped to address this problem. The most common devices today include cell phones and laptops. More than 90% of U.S. citizens have access to the internet in some way and that figure is slowly increasing. Across the world, the internet is becoming more available than ever with Africa being the only place where internet service is scarce.
