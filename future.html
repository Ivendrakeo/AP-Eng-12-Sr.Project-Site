Web 2.0

Web 2.0 is how in modern day, the World Wide Web emphasizes user-generated content, usability, and productivity for standard consumers. It does not refer to an update to any technical aspect of the internet, rather, a modern changes in the way Web pages are designed and used.

Internet of Things

Having the internet available on kitchen appliances has only recently become available, but will likley continue growing and expanding to all household appliances. The benefit of linking appliances to the internet is that they can be monitored and changed on any other device that is connected to the internet. This allows interesting possibilities such creating a shopping list on a fridge and viewing it from the store or de-thawing a specific package of meat for dinner later.

Li-Fi

Li-Fi is an alternative way of transmitting internet by using light waves. Traditional Wi-Fi uses radio waves and the benefit of light over sound is that light waves are quicker and able to transmit more bandwidth (Sakshi & Khandal). It also has the added effect of creating light which the developers hope to use in order to replace existing home lighting with their integrated Li-Fi bulbs.
