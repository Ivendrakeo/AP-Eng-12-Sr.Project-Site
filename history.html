Begining

The history of the Internet begins with the development of electronic computers in the 1950s. Initial concepts of packet switching technologies were contracted by the 1960s for network systems, including the development of the ARPA internet project. The first message was sent over a computer from science Professor Leonard Kleinrock's laboratory at University of California to the second network node at Stanford Research Institute

Development

Eventually the research sharing system took off and its support skyrocketed. By 1972 E-mail was developed and used to transmit messages over long distances quickly. Protocols followed suite shortly after and defined the rules and procedures of the internet. The first included TCP/IP in 1800. By 1986, One of the biggest early developments included the Domain Name System [DNS] and IP distribution which provided unique device and site identification.
